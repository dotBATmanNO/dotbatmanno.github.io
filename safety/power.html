⚡ Power Outage – Norway

Here’s what to do if the power goes out in Norway, whether it’s a short blackout or a longer emergency.

🔌 Immediate Actions

  • Check if the outage is local (e.g. fuse blown) or widespread.
  • Turn off sensitive electronics to avoid damage when power returns.
  • Use flashlights — avoid candles due to fire risk.
  • Preserve phone battery and mobile data.

📡 Stay Informed

  • Use battery-powered radio or emergency apps for updates.
  • Check Elvia or your local grid provider for outage maps.
  • Follow DSB and Regjeringen.no for national alerts.

🥶 Cold Weather Tips

  • Layer clothing and use blankets to retain heat.
  • Close doors to unused rooms.
  • Use safe heating sources — avoid indoor use of grills or fuel burners.

🧊 Food and Water

  • Keep fridge/freezer closed to preserve food longer.
  • Use stored water if pumps stop working.
  • Have a manual can opener and ready-to-eat supplies.

🧭 Emergency Preparedness

  • Have a power bank, flashlight, batteries, and basic supplies ready.
  • Consider a backup heat source or generator (used safely).
  • Check on neighbors, especially elderly or vulnerable.
